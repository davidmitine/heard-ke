Heard.ke: Privacy Policy & Terms of Use

Last updated: 18 July 2026  ·  Effective date: August 2026

This Privacy Policy and Terms of Use ("Policy") governs your use of Heard.ke (www.heard.co.ke), a private space for men in Kenya to release what's weighing on them and connect around shared interests. By using Heard.ke, you agree to the terms below. If you don't agree, please don't use the site.

1. What Heard.ke Is (and Isn't)

Heard.ke is a peer support and self-expression space. It is not a clinical, medical, or therapeutic service, and nothing on this site is a substitute for professional care. We don't diagnose, treat, or advise. We give men a place to speak, and a community built around doing things together.

If you are in crisis or thinking about harming yourself, please see Section 7 before doing anything else.

2. No Accounts, No Names, No Data Capture

Heard.ke is built so there's nothing of yours for us to lose. This applies fully to The Release. The one narrow exception, event RSVPs, is covered in Section 2.3.

  • No sign-up. There is no account creation, login, or app download.
  • No identity required. We never ask for your name, email address, or phone number to use "The Release" or browse the community space.
  • No content on our servers. When you write, speak, or draw in The Release, that content is processed and stored on your own device. If you choose to "burn" it, it is deleted from your device and was never transmitted to us. If you choose to "seal" or "save" it, it stays on your device, under your control, until you delete it yourself.
  • We do not read, store, sell, or share the contents of your Release, because we never receive them in the first place.

We built it this way on purpose. It shapes how every feature on the site works.

FeatureWhere it goes

2.1 What limited technical data exists

To keep any website running and secure, our hosting infrastructure automatically and briefly logs standard technical information, for example IP address, browser type, and timestamp of the page request. Heard.ke also uses a small number of third-party embeds on the live site (for example, event or mapping widgets) to support specific features like RSVPs; these embeds may set their own cookies or logs, governed by their own providers' privacy terms, not by us. Our own technical data collection is:

  • Not linked to any name, account, or identity, except where you've provided contact details for an event RSVP as described in Section 2.3.
  • Not used to build a profile of you.
  • Retained only as long as needed for security, abuse prevention, and keeping the site online, then deleted.

If we ever introduce optional, aggregated, anonymous analytics (for example, to see how many people visit a page, not who they are), we will update this Policy first and design it so no individual can be identified. As of the effective date above, no such analytics are active on the core Release flow.

2.2 Community and interest-based features

If Heard.ke offers community spaces built around shared interests, participation there is optional and separate from The Release. Anything you choose to post in a community space is visible to other members as you set it. You decide what to post; we don't collect it any other way. Section 5 covers your responsibility for what you post.

2.3 Event RSVPs

Where Heard.ke hosts an in-person or community event, you may be asked for a phone number or email address to RSVP. This is the one place on Heard.ke where we ask for contact details, and it exists solely so we can confirm your spot and send you information about that event.

  • We use it only for that event, and only to manage attendance and send event-related updates.
  • We do not link it to anything you write in The Release, and we do not build a profile from it.
  • We do not use it for marketing, sell it, or share it with anyone outside what's needed to run the event.
  • If you'd rather not share contact details, you're welcome to skip RSVP-only events.

3. Data Protection Act, 2019 (Kenya)

Heard.ke is designed to minimise data processing to the point where, for most users, no personal data is ever collected. Where any technical data is processed as described in Section 2.1, or where you provide a phone number or email address to RSVP to an event as described in Section 2.3, we act as a data controller under the Data Protection Act, 2019, and process it lawfully, fairly, and only for the limited purposes stated here. You may contact us using the details in Section 9 with any question about this.

4. Not a Crisis or Emergency Service

Heard.ke provides relief and connection, but it does not monitor users in real time, does not intervene in emergencies, and cannot see what you write in The Release. If you or someone else is in immediate danger, Heard.ke is not equipped to help in that moment. Please contact:

Emergency Services (Kenya): 999 / 112

The same applies anywhere else on the site where distress or self-harm comes up.

5. Your Content, Your Responsibility

Anything you write, speak, draw, or post through Heard.ke, whether in The Release or in a community space, is created and shared entirely at your own discretion.

  • You are solely responsible for what you share and for making sure it doesn't violate any law or infringe anyone else's rights.
  • Because content in The Release is never transmitted to or stored by us, we have no ability to access, moderate, or remove it. Responsibility for it rests with you and your device alone.
  • For community spaces, moderation guidelines apply as set out separately. Even so, Heard.ke does not pre-screen every post and is not responsible for content posted by other users.
  • Heard.ke is not liable for outcomes, decisions, or actions you take as a result of using the platform. Section 6 sets out the exact limits of that.
